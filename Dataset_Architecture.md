3. North leads total profit with $2,580.20; protect this region's operating playbook and replicate it elsewhere.
4. South has the lowest regional margin at 34.9%; review discounting, product mix, and fulfillment cost.
5. Furniture is the largest category by revenue at $6,531.11.
6. Furniture contributes the most category profit at $2,619.70.
7. Office has the weakest category margin at 29.9%; prioritize margin repair before scaling promotions.
8. Shoe Storage Bench is the top product by revenue at $2,262.00; consider featuring it in hero placements and CRM campaigns.
9. RetailPro Duluth Supercenter is the highest revenue store in the sample with $3,623.38; use it as a benchmark for local execution.
10. Web is the highest revenue channel at $6,507.75; monitor capacity and customer experience in this channel.
11. Value Seeker customers generate the largest segment revenue at $6,032.98; campaign planning should prioritize this audience.
12. Average order value is $470.45; raising attachment rate by one item could materially improve revenue without new customer acquisition.
13. The repeat customer rate is 56.2%; second-purchase journeys should be a CRM priority.
14. Return amount totals $3,696.16, equal to 15.7% of sales; returns should be reviewed in category and fulfillment meetings.
15. Furniture is the largest return-cost category at $1,068.15; inspect product detail pages, sizing, quality, and packaging.
16. The leading return reason by value is 'Incorrect item' with $792.14; assign an owner to reduce this controllable leakage.
17. 3 inventory rows are marked Critical and require immediate replenishment or transfer decisions.
18. 11 inventory rows are below reorder thresholds; these should be included in the next replenishment cycle.
19. 4 inventory rows are flagged as Overstock; consider transfers, markdowns, or reduced purchasing.
20. 32 inventory rows are Healthy, giving operations a baseline for expected stock productivity.
21. Regional revenue concentration shows North at 25.5%; avoid overdependence by developing underpenetrated regions.
22. Profit margin variance between the best and weakest regions is an executive opportunity for pricing and assortment governance.
23. Product ranking should be reviewed together with return rate, because high revenue products can still erode margin through refunds.
24. Customer loyalty tier analysis should be paired with retention rate to distinguish true loyalty from one-time promotional purchases.
25. Inventory health and sales velocity should be reviewed together so replenishment favors profitable, fast-moving products rather than only low-stock items.
26. Forecasting and target achievement should be interpreted alongside margin so revenue growth does not mask profit deterioration.
